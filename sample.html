Ashenda Festival in Tigray

About the Festival

The Ashenda Festival is an annual Tigrayan celebration that honors women and girls. It has its origins in the Axum region of Tigray, dating back to around 400 AD. The festival is typically held in August and is the largest in Mekelle, the regional capital. During Ashenda, women and girls actively participate in various festivities, while men often take on roles such as gift-giving and protection.

Cultural Significance

Ashenda is a cultural celebration that not only highlights the creativity and talents of Tigrayan women and girls but also pays tribute to the Virgin Mary. It follows two weeks of Filseta, during which fasting is observed, further emphasizing the religious and spiritual aspects of the festival.
